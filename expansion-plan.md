# IPR Education Game Expansion Plan

## Current Status (MVP)
- Trademark recognition activity for cars
- Basic visual feedback system
- Educational explanations

## Phase 1: Complete Core IPR Modules

### 1. Copyright Module
**Objective**: Teach students to identify copyright infringement
**Activities**:
- Music identification challenge (recognizing unauthorized use)
- Book cover matching (identifying pirated copies)
- Artwork plagiarism detection
**Visual Elements**:
- Side-by-side comparison of original vs. copied works
- Timeline activities showing creation dates
**Educational Content**:
- Explanation of what can be copyrighted
- Duration of copyright protection
- Difference between inspiration and infringement

### 2. Patent Module
**Objective**: Help students understand patentable inventions
**Activities**:
- Invention evaluation (novelty, non-obviousness, utility)
- Prior art search simulations
- Patent claim interpretation
**Visual Elements**:
- Interactive invention disclosure forms
- Patent drawing comparisons
- Timeline of invention development
**Educational Content**:
- Patent application process overview
- Types of patents (utility, design, plant)
- Patent term and expiration

### 3. Design Module
**Objective**: Recognize protected industrial designs
**Activities**:
- Ornamental design identification
- Design similarity assessments
- Function vs. form distinctions
**Visual Elements**:
- 3D model comparisons
- Design evolution timelines
- Ornamental vs. functional feature highlighting
**Educational Content**:
- Design patent vs. utility patent differences
- Protection scope of design rights
- International design protection systems

## Phase 2: Game Progression System

### Level Structure
1. **Basic Level**
   - Simple identification tasks
   - Clear right/wrong scenarios
   - Immediate feedback with explanations

2. **Intermediate Level**
   - Complex scenarios with multiple correct answers
   - Time pressure elements
   - Comparative analysis tasks

3. **Advanced Level**
   - Real-world case studies
   - Multi-step reasoning problems
   - Policy and ethical considerations

### Progression Features
- **Scoreboard**: Track points for correct answers
- **Progress Map**: Visual representation of completed modules
- **Achievement Badges**: Recognition for milestones
- **Factual Titbits**: Pop-up interesting facts about IP
- **Quick Recap**: Summary screen at end of each module

## Learning Outcomes Matrix

| IPR Type | Basic Level | Intermediate Level | Advanced Level |
|----------|-------------|-------------------|----------------|
| Trademarks | Identify logo misuse | Analyze brand confusion | Evaluate trademark strength |
| Copyrights | Recognize direct copying | Assess fair use scenarios | Analyze licensing issues |
| Patents | Distinguish inventions | Evaluate patentability | Interpret claims |
| Designs | Identify ornamental features | Compare design similarity | Assess design freedom |

## Expected Impact
Upon completion of the full game, students will be able to:
1. Define and distinguish between different types of intellectual property
2. Identify instances of IP infringement in everyday life
3. Understand the importance of IP protection for innovation
4. Apply basic IP concepts to real-world scenarios
5. Develop critical thinking skills for IP-related decisions
